Founder effect¶

When a new population is founded it is quite common that just a few members of an older original population create the new population. This causes a bottleneck that has an impact in the diversity of the new population and the genetic distance with the original populations.

This is a common phenomenon that happens, for instance, when few individuals colonize a new island or territory or are used to create a new breed.
